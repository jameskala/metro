Riyadh Metro: Complete Guide 2025 – Routes, Stations, Tickets & Map

The Riyadh Metro is one of the most transformative public transportation projects in Saudi Arabia, designed to modernize daily travel, support economic growth, and enhance sustainability under Vision 2030. Once fully operational, it is expected to serve millions of passengers while reducing traffic congestion and improving mobility across the capital.

Overview of the Riyadh Metro Project

The metro network spans 176 km with 85 modern stations across 6 color-coded lines. Stations feature air-conditioning, elevators, digital screens, and accessibility options for people with disabilities.

Riyadh Metro Lines

  • Blue Line (Line 1): Olaya & King Fahd Road Corridor
  • Red Line (Line 2): King Abdullah Road Corridor
  • Orange Line (Line 3): Al Madinah Al Munawarah Road to Al Khalidiyah
  • Yellow Line (Line 4): King Khalid International Airport Corridor
  • Green Line (Line 5): King Abdulaziz Road Corridor
  • Purple Line (Line 6): Abdulrahman Bin Auf & Sheikh Hassan Bin Hussain Road

Ticketing and Smart Travel System

The Metro will use a unified electronic fare system called the NOL Card, which works across buses and metro stations. Passengers can top up via kiosks, mobile apps, and online portals.

Timings (Expected)

  • Weekdays: 5:30 AM – 12:00 AM
  • Weekends: 6:00 AM – 2:00 AM

Key Benefits of the Riyadh Metro

  • Reduced traffic congestion
  • Eco-friendly transportation
  • Fast connectivity between residential & commercial hubs
  • Supports Vision 2030 smart city development

Major Stations

  • King Abdullah Financial District (KAFD)
  • King Khalid International Airport
  • Diplomatic Quarter
  • King Saud University
  • Olaya, Malaz & Batha Transit Hubs

Future Plans

The metro will integrate with bus networks and park-and-ride stations, creating a unified and hassle-free public transportation ecosystem.

Conclusion

The Riyadh Metro will redefine daily travel in the capital, offering fast, clean, affordable, and convenient mobility for residents, tourists, students, and workers. As Saudi Arabia continues to transform under Vision 2030, the metro stands as a symbol of progress and innovation.
